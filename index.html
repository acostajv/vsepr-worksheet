01Drawing Lewis Structures▼
Why Lewis structures?

In VSEPR theory, the 3D shape of a molecule is determined by how many electron groups surround the central atom — they repel each other and spread as far apart as possible. Before we can predict shape, we need to know where all the electrons are. That's what a Lewis structure shows us.

Once you've drawn it, you'll count the electron groups on the central atom and use that number throughout the rest of this activity.

What counts as 1 electron group?
Single bond= 1 group
Double bond= 1 group
Triple bond= 1 group
Lone pair= 1 group
Any bond to one neighbor counts as one group — bond order (the number of bonds between two atoms: single = 1, double = 2, triple = 3) doesn't matter for counting groups. Lone pairs on the central atom count too. Count central atom only.

How to Draw a Lewis Structure
1
Count Total Valence Electrons
Add valence electrons for every atom. Use the group number from the periodic table — the A-group number equals valence electrons:
Group 1A = 1 e⁻  ·  Group 2A = 2 e⁻
Group 3A = 3 e⁻  ·  Group 4A = 4 e⁻
Group 5A = 5 e⁻  ·  Group 6A = 6 e⁻  ·  Group 7A = 7 e⁻
2
Identify the Central Atom
Usually the atom there is only one of, or the least electronegative. Carbon always goes in the middle — hydrogen never goes in the middle.
3
Connect Atoms with Single Bonds
Place outer atoms around the central atom and connect each with one bond (= 2 electrons). Subtract those from your total.
4
Distribute Remaining Electrons
Add lone pairs to outer atoms first to satisfy octets (H only needs 2). Use any leftover electrons on the central atom.
5
Check for Double / Triple Bonds
Does every atom have a full octet? If the central atom is short, move a lone pair from a neighbor to form a double bond. Never add new electrons.
Drawing tip: Draw bonds as lines and lone pairs as dots or small circles. Color-code to tell them apart. Tap ↩️ to undo a stroke.
⚠ Octet rule exceptions — only ever on the central atom. Outer atoms always obey the octet rule.

BF₃ — Incomplete Octet
Boron (Group 3A) forms 3 bonds = only 6 electrons. This is stable — do not add a double bond.

PCl₅, SF₆, ClF₃, BrF₅, XeF₄ — Expanded Octet
Period 3 and beyond (P, S, Cl, Br, Xe) have d orbitals available and can hold more than 8 electrons. Just connect all outer atoms with single bonds — exceeding 8 is correct here.
